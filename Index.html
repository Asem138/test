The Seven Wonders Of The World

The Seven Wonders of the Ancient World are a list of remarkable constructions known for their extraordinary architecture, art, and engineering. Here's a summary of these wonders:

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon:
Legendary terraced gardens said to be built in ancient Babylon. 		The Statue of Zeus at Olympia:
A monumental statue of Zeus, crafted in gold and ivory. 		The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus:
A grand temple dedicated to the goddess Artemis in Ephesus.
The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus:
An elaborate tomb built for Mausolus, a Persian satrap. 		The Colossus of Rhodes:
An elaborate tomb built for Mausolus, a Persian satrap. 		The Lighthouse of Alexandria:
An ancient lighthouse that guided ships safely to Alexandria's harbor.
The Great Pyramid of Giza:
The oldest and only surviving wonder, built as a tomb for Pharaoh Khufu.
